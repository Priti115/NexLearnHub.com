Anatomy

Anatomy is the scientific study of the structure and organization of living organisms. It involves the examination of various components, such as organs, tissues, and systems, providing a comprehensive understanding of the physical form and spatial relationships within the body.

Physiology

Physiology is the branch of biology that investigates the functions and processes of living organisms. It explores the mechanisms that enable life, examining how cells, tissues, and organs work together to maintain homeostasis, respond to stimuli, and carry out essential biological processes.

Psychiatry

Psychiatry is a medical specialty that focuses on mental health and disorders. It involves the diagnosis, treatment, and prevention of conditions affecting mental well-being, encompassing a range of emotional, behavioral, and cognitive disorders.

Pharmacology

Pharmacology is the scientific study of drugs and their interactions with living organisms. It delves into the mechanisms of drug action, their effects on physiological processes, and their therapeutic applications in treating diseases or promoting health.

Immunology

Immunology is a branch of biology dedicated to the study of the immune system. It explores the body's defense mechanisms against pathogens, the cellular and molecular components of immunity, and the mechanisms underlying immune responses and disorders.

Surgery

Surgery is a medical discipline that utilizes operative procedures for the diagnosis, treatment, or cure of various conditions, injuries, or diseases in living organisms. Surgeons employ manual or instrumental techniques to address structural or functional abnormalities within the body.

Genetics

Genetics is the scientific study of genes, heredity, and genetic variations in living organisms. It examines the inheritance of traits, the structure and function of genes, and the molecular basis of genetic diversity within populations.

Reproductive biology

Reproductive Biology focuses on the study of reproductive systems, processes, and behaviors in living organisms. It encompasses gametogenesis, fertilization, embryonic development, and reproductive strategies, contributing to an understanding of how species perpetuate and evolve.

Microbiology

Microbiology is the branch of biology that investigates microorganisms, including bacteria, viruses, and fungi. It explores their structure, function, genetics, and roles in diverse environments, playing a crucial role in fields such as medicine, agriculture, and environmental science.

Paediatrics

Pediatrics focuses on the health and medical care of infants, children, and adolescents, addressing their physical, emotional, and social development from birth to young adulthood.

Dermatology

Dermatology specializes in diagnosing and treating skin conditions, including diseases, infections, and cosmetic issues, ensuring the health and appearance of the skin, hair, and nails.

Ophthalmology

Ophthalmology is the branch of medicine dedicated to eye care, encompassing the diagnosis, treatment, and prevention of eye diseases, vision problems, and surgical procedures to maintain eye health.
